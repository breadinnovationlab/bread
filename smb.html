Small Business, Start-Ups, Non-Profits

1.

Do
More

Most organizations are focused on the core functions that are central to their business. Rarely do we take the time to strategize the crucial technological elements of team productivity.

In phase one, we'll partner up and take a step back to assess the technology that supports the work and workers of your projects and organizations.

2.

Work
Smarter

With modernized technology, we'll leverage work flow design to create transparency and open communication across all work, tasks, goals, projects, and people. By moving all work from our own personal silos into a public square of sorts, we can minimize the need for meetings while maximizing efficiency and communication.

Phase two is all about bringing the team and their work together.

3.

Reduce
Burnout

Having the work and workers transparently operating in the same place and method means fewer meetings, phone calls, texts, and hassle.

With more bandwith, decision-making and governance naturally shifts and we can begin to intentionally cultivate a workplace culture that nurtures critical thinking and self agency.
